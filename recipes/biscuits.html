Description

This buscuit recipie comes from the ranches in the Teton valley of Wyoming. It was originaly found in a cook book of local foods. These where originaly made in a dutch oven on a fire for cowboys while out on the range.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups Flour
  • 4 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 0.25 tsp salt
  • 1 stick of cold butter
  • 1 egg
  • 2/3 cup milk

Steps

  1. Mix all dry ingredients in a mixing bowl.
  2. Use a pastry knife to cut in the butter. You want the butter chopped up into small pieces and mixed in with all the dry ingredients.
  3. Make a crater in the mixture and crack an egg into it and add the milk. Beat the egg and milk then procede to fold in the flower mixture. At this point the dough will get stiff so I usually use my hands to mix.
  4. Once the dough is mixed start tearing off handfulls of dough and form them into a rough ball and place them on an ungreased baking sheet
  5. Cook for 10 to 12 minuts at 425
